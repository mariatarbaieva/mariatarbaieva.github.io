Free Trial

Company: Cobalt
Role: Lead designer

The free trial initiative aims to facilitate product-led growth for Cobalt while also reducing the Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) for the business. This is accomplished by minimizing the need for communication between prospects, Business Development Representatives (BDRs) and Sales.

Problem

User needs to compare security vendors, their tools and ways of working understand a scope to test and provide a pricing to budget approvers but there is no information they can get instantly without contact with sales because most of the security platforms do not provide transparent pricing and access to the platform.

User research

User research activities at Cobalt are distributed across product, design, and product marketing teams. With marketing responsible for personas, they provided us with a comprehensive understanding of these personas to enabled us to define research candidates that align with the specific personas we were targeting.
Among several user types we focused on following: Technical Leader and Decision Maker

❮ ❯

We organized collaborative work sessions involving internal stakeholders such as Marketing, Sales, BDR, Sales Operations, Engineering and notably, the security team. These sessions were aimed at defining the workflow process.

Following that stage, our recruitment initiatives were initiated. We received support from other members of the Cobalt team to identify actual prospective candidates for interviews. This collaborative assistance was instrumental in obtaining valuable insights and comprehending prospects jobs to be done.

I've initiated the prototyping phase and implemented the ideas that we collectively generated as a team. The primary insights were:

  • • Utilize the empty state to educate prospects

  • • Lead users through the product. We used Pendo for this purpose

  • • Accelerate the initial development phase and employ Pendo for pop-ups and tooltips

  • • Incorporate product videos to provide users with a visual representation of potential pentest outcomes

  • • Utilize screenshots of the demo environment to enable users to preview the potential appearance of pentest results

After first prototype was ready. We conducted interviews with potential users and recorded the insights within our user research repository. In total, I made 5 prototype iterations based on user feedback.

Result

Ater 5 iterations, we arrived at a prototype that we implemented and continued to enhance using insights from Hotjar, Pendo, and user feedback data.

Key Learnings

After a launch we learned following:

  • • Pricing component was confusing to the users. Communication with sales was still essential to close the deal

  • • Some of the customers that didnt have an account in platform also tried to sign up and access an app

  • • People expected to start pentest immediately after signing up
